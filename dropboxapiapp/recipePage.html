Flaky Pastry Pie Crust

Shared by Donna Feldman

Ingredients

1.5 cups all-purpose flour; more for your workspace

1/2 cup cake flour

1-3 tablespoons sugar (less for savory pies)

1 teaspoon salt

3/4 cup (1 1/2 sticks) cold butter, cut into 1/2-inch cubes

1/2 cup water, chilled

1/4 cup vegetable oil, chilled

Instructions

  • Put all the dry ingredients into a food processor and pulse briefly to combine.
  • Add the cubes of butter, and pulse for 3 seconds at a time, until the butter is the size of peas. The dough should stick together slightly if you squeeze it with your fingers.
  • With the food processor running, slowly pour in the water and vegetable oil. Stop processing when the dough balls up.
  • On a well-floured surface, dump the dough into one pile. Using the heel of your hand, scrape the dough bit by bit into a single ball of dough (you should be able to see butter smears in the dough as you do this; if you can't, you blended it too much in the food processor.
  • Gather up the dough, and wrap it in wax paper.
  • Refrigerate for at least one day before using. When rolling out to use in a pie, only roll out one half at a time, keeping the other one chilled in the fridge.
    • Recipe makes enough for two bottom pie shells, or a top and a bottom.
